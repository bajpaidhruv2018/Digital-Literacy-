# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### ✅ The Do's
1. **Do showcase your projects:** Treat platforms like X (Twitter) and LinkedIn as a public portfolio to build in public and share your GitHub commits.
2. **Do curate your network:** Follow industry leaders, senior engineers, and companies you actually want to work for. 
3. **Do pause before posting:** Always assume recruiters and professors can see everything you post, even on private accounts.
4. **Do use 2FA:** Protect your accounts by enabling two-factor authentication (preferably via an authenticator app, not SMS).
5. **Do fact-check before amplifying:** Verify the source before retweeting or sharing sensational news or tech rumors.

### ❌ The Don'ts
1. **Don't vent about professors or peers:** Never complain about your university, coursework, or team members publicly. It leaves a permanent negative footprint.
2. **Don't leak sensitive data:** Never accidentally post screenshots that contain API keys, passwords, or personal identifying information.
3. **Don't engage in flame wars:** Getting into toxic arguments in comment sections is a waste of time and destroys your professional vibe.
4. **Don't overshare your real-time location:** Protect your physical safety by posting event photos *after* you have left the venue.
5. **Don't rely solely on privacy settings:** Platforms update their policies constantly; assume anything uploaded to the internet is permanent and public.
